What is i-Account (Virtual Account)?

Main Point: i-Account is a unique Virtual Account for a company under KDD’s bank account in Vietnam, created for collection/prefunding, which accepts VND. We help clients set up a LocalVND wallet (can we?) to record fund transactions, and clients can initiate payouts via the wallet.

Or: i-Account is an upgraded account of LocalVND, allowing clients to prefund their wallet in multiple currencies (e.g., VNDA) and request payouts in VND.

Sub Point: Please check which one is easier to set up. The first one is the default meaning of a virtual account.

What are the KYC requirements?

  • Company address

What is the difference between a LocalVND account and an i-account?

LocalVND Account: LocalVND is like an exchange, allowing users to buy and sell crypto/fiat.

i-Account: i-Account acts like a virtual bank account for a Vietnam bank. Instead of holding a real bank account, a LocalVND wallet is created, and it tops up VNDA (instead of VND).

Targeting Groups:

  • E-commerce
  • Foreigners living in Vietnam
  • Vietnamese people overseas
  • Businesses paying salaries
  • Workers for daily spending
How do deposit and payout work?

Deposit: We provide bank account details, and users can top-up their LocalVND wallet.

Payout: Payout is done via VNPay, but merchants can initiate the payout using LocalVND.

How can clients without a Vietnam bank account receive payouts?

Clients still need a Vietnam bank account to receive payouts. However:

Solution 1: Deposit other currencies (MYR, USD, SGD, HKD, THB) and convert to VNDA. Clients can request a payout anytime.

Solution 2: If the recipient does not hold a Vietnam bank account, they can register with a LocalVND account using a Tron wallet, and we will transfer the funds to their wallet.

What are the features of an i-account?

i-Accounts accept both fiat and cryptocurrency, enabling seamless financial transactions within Vietnam and internationally.

Is a report available to record client transactions?

Yes, reports are available to record all client transactions, including deposits and payouts.
